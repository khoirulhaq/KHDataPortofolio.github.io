A study of transfer learning in digital rock properties measurement

A study exploring the use of transfer learning with convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for predicting physical parameters of porous rock, essential in hydrocarbon exploration. Utilizing six pre-trained models, including ResNet152 and DenseNet201, the research demonstrated that DenseNet201 achieved the best performance with a 2.11% error rate. This approach highlights the potential for more efficient and cost-effective analysis compared to traditional laboratory or digital rock methods.

Machine Learning Application of Two-Dimensional Fracture Properties Estimation

Research on predicting mean aperture and surface roughness in fractures, key parameters influencing permeability in petroleum reservoirs, using a machine learning algorithm based on Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). By leveraging image processing for dataset generation, the study demonstrates that CNN models achieve accurate predictions with efficient computational performance, offering a robust tool for fracture geometry analysis.

Machine learning prediction of tortuosity in digital rock

Research on predicting tortuosity, a key property of porous materials indicating fluid flow complexity, using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). By training CNN models on digital rock samples with simulated tortuosity values, the study demonstrates accurate predictions, with the Xception model achieving the best performance (lowest RMSE: 0.90962), offering a faster alternative to conventional laboratory and numerical methods.
